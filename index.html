Confirmed cases vs. estimates of actual infections

Confirmed cases are only a (small and biased) subsample of actual infections. How small and how biased? It depends on the testing resources and protocols applied in each country/region.

Moreover the number of confirmed and notified deaths is also an estimate or actual deaths caused by the virus. And specially in some countries/regions this value could be underestimated. Statistics on mortality monitoring on all causes, as project EuroMomo does, can be useful to infer alternative (and better) estimates based on excess mortality. But it seems that Covid-19 could be less lethal in relative terms than current figures we get in Italy or Spain (confirmed deaths/confirmed cases vs estimated deaths/estimated infections). Though much more contagious than we expected. So much more lethal in absolute terms. With the aggravating circumstance of saturating healthcare systems and increasing even more the number of direct and indirect deaths caused. Any confirmed cases and alternative kind of estimates of actual infections and deaths should be understood under their context. Language should be used rigourously to avoid misinformation.

I personally recommend analysis and data curated by Our World in Data. Thanks to Max Roser et al. I also appreciate their effort to persuade general press to use adequate terms. And to promote curated analysis from different sources:

Simulation model

13 parameter model simulation of Covid-19 evolution on Region of Madrid (Spain) . This calculator implements a classical infectious disease model: SEIR (Susceptible â†’ Exposed â†’ Infected â†’ Removed) [Wu, et. al, Kucharski et. al].

I tried to adjust the evolution of confirmed deaths (Fatalities) in Madrid since the day this number was over N=10. Displacing Intervention day relatively to this reference. And ignoring the Day 0 as time origin. I expect that following trends of confirmed fatalities would propagate less uncertainty and provide more reliable estimates than starting from the first confirmed case.
